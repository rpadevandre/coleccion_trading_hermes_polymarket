# Validation Plan — Restaurant Catering Follow-Up Copilot

## Validation thesis

Independent restaurants and small groups lose catering revenue because follow-up is inconsistent. A lightweight copilot that prioritizes quote follow-up, post-event thank-yous, lapsed account reactivation, and seasonal reminders can justify a paid pilot if it ties work to recovered revenue.

## Ideal buyer

- Restaurant owner/operator, catering manager, or GM.
- 1-10 locations, English-speaking markets first: US, Canada, UK, Australia.
- Already receives catering orders or inquiries from offices, schools, clinics, nonprofits, real estate teams, churches, and local businesses.
- Average catering order value ideally above $250.
- Has at least 20 catering contacts/orders from the last 90 days.

## Money-focused offers to test

1. **Catering follow-up audit** — $99-$250; analyze last 90 days of quotes/orders and produce prioritized follow-up list.
2. **30-day recovered revenue pilot** — $500-$1,500; import data, create follow-up queue, weekly report, staff-approved message drafts.
3. **Monthly follow-up engine** — $300-$1,000/location/month depending on volume.
4. **Seasonal campaign add-on** — $250-$750 for holiday/event reactivation campaign.

## Success metrics

- 20 owner/operator conversations in 21 days.
- 5 restaurants share anonymized/exported catering inquiry/order data or manually enter sample records.
- 3 paid audits or pilots.
- At least one pilot shows recovered or influenced revenue greater than pilot price.
- Staff can process daily follow-up queue in under 20 minutes.

## Outreach experiment

Target restaurants with visible catering menus and business lunch/office catering positioning.

Personalized hook examples:
- "I noticed you have catering packages for office lunches. Do you have a system for following up with companies that ordered once but have not reordered?"
- "If we found 20 old catering contacts worth following up this week, would that be useful?"
- "We are testing a 30-day recovered-catering-revenue pilot for restaurants with existing catering demand."

Channels:
- Email to owner/operator/catering manager.
- Phone call during off-peak hours.
- Local chamber/business association intros.
- Partnerships with restaurant web/POS consultants.

## Interview questions

- How many catering inquiries did you get last month?
- How many quotes do not turn into orders?
- Who follows up after an event?
- Do you know which catering customers should reorder monthly/quarterly?
- What is an average catering order worth?
- What would a recovered order be worth to you?
- Would you pay for a 30-day pilot if it generated approved follow-up lists and scripts?

## Smoke test landing page

CTA: "Get a 30-day catering follow-up pilot."

Lead magnet: "Upload/export your last 90 days of catering inquiries; we return a prioritized follow-up map."

Qualification fields:
- average order value,
- monthly inquiries,
- location count,
- current follow-up owner,
- catering menu URL,
- preferred pilot start date.

## Disqualifiers

- Restaurants with no catering offer or very low average order value.
- Teams requiring fully automated sends before proving message quality.
- Prospects unwilling to approve messages or respect opt-outs.
- Chains requiring complex enterprise integrations before pilot validation.
