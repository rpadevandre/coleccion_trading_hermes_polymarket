# Property Maintenance Triage — Public Content (EN)

## Positioning

Property Maintenance Triage helps small property managers turn messy tenant maintenance texts into structured, vendor-ready work orders.

## Audience

- Independent property managers.
- Landlords with 20-300 doors.
- Small multifamily operators.
- Maintenance coordinators handling requests across SMS, email, and portals.

## Public narrative

Most maintenance problems do not start as clean work orders. They start as vague messages: “the sink is leaking,” “the AC is broken,” or “there is a weird smell.” The hidden cost is the back-and-forth needed to figure out urgency, collect photos, route the right vendor, and document the response.

The first version focuses on one practical promise: make the first maintenance touch cleaner and faster, without replacing the property-management system.

## Content themes

1. **The cost of vague maintenance requests** — time lost before a vendor can act.
2. **Emergency vs urgent vs routine** — why consistent triage protects managers and tenants.
3. **Photo prompts that save truck rolls** — getting evidence before dispatch.
4. **Maintenance audit trails** — why documentation matters when tenants dispute timing or outcomes.
5. **Small operator leverage** — better process without hiring another coordinator.

## Example LinkedIn post

> A tenant saying “there is water under the sink” is not a work order yet.
>
> A good maintenance process needs to know: active leak or old stain? Which sink? Photos? Permission to enter? Any visible damage? Is a plumber needed now or can this wait?
>
> Property Maintenance Triage is a small AI-assisted layer for turning vague tenant reports into reviewable work orders before they consume a manager’s afternoon.

## Lead magnet idea

**20-Request Maintenance Triage Audit**

A property manager sends sanitized examples of recent maintenance messages. The audit returns category, urgency, missing information, vendor type, tenant response draft, and estimated time saved.
