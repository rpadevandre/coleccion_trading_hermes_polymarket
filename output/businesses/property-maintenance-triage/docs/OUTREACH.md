# Outreach — Property Maintenance Triage

## ICP

- English-speaking property managers with 20-300 doors.
- Lean teams receiving tenant requests via text, email, portal and phone.
- Buyers: owner-operator, head of property management, maintenance coordinator, small multifamily principal.
- Exclude: enterprise PM firms with mature maintenance platforms, operators unwilling to use synthetic data in early validation, portfolios with very low maintenance volume.

## Money Question

"If this cut maintenance back-and-forth by 30% and helped your team avoid one missed emergency escalation, would it justify $149-$499/month?"

## Cold Email

Subject: maintenance triage for {{company_name}}

Hi {{first_name}},

I am validating a lightweight maintenance triage workflow for small property managers. It turns vague tenant messages into urgency labels, missing-info prompts and vendor-ready work-order drafts.

The pilot is not a replacement for your property management software. It is a focused inbox layer for faster classification and cleaner dispatch.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call to pressure-test whether this would save your team enough time to matter?

Best,
{{sender}}

## LinkedIn DM

Quick question: how does your team triage tenant maintenance requests after hours or when messages are vague? I am testing a small workflow that classifies urgency and drafts vendor-ready summaries. Looking for blunt feedback from operators with 20-300 doors.

## Discovery Questions

1. How many maintenance requests hit your team each week?
2. What channels do tenants use most: text, email, portal, phone?
3. Which categories create the most expensive mistakes?
4. How do you define emergency vs urgent vs routine?
5. How often do vendors ask for details that tenants did not provide?
6. What is the cost of a delayed emergency escalation?
7. What PM software do you use today, and where does intake break down?
8. Would you pay for an inbox layer if it reduced follow-up and improved documentation?

## Validation Channels

- Local property manager associations.
- Landlord and small multifamily operator forums.
- LinkedIn outreach to maintenance coordinators.
- Property management consultants.
- Operators publishing rental portfolio growth content.

## No-Go Signals

- Buyer only wants a full property management suite.
- Existing PM software already solves intake/triage well enough.
- Maintenance volume is too low to justify spend.
- Buyer will not pilot without live tenant PII before compliance work.
