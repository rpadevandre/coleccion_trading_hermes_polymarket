# Landing Copy — Property Maintenance Triage

## Primary Headline

Turn vague tenant maintenance texts into vendor-ready work orders.

## Subheadline

An AI-assisted intake and triage queue for small property managers that classifies urgency, asks the right follow-up questions and drafts dispatch-ready work orders before your team loses the thread.

## Target Buyer

Small property managers, landlords with 20-300 doors and lean multifamily operators in English-speaking markets.

## Above-the-Fold CTA

- Primary: `Audit your maintenance inbox`
- Secondary: `See the triage workflow`

## Problem Bullets

- Tenants report problems in vague fragments: "leak," "no heat," "outlet sparking," "fridge broken."
- Managers waste time asking the same follow-up questions and deciding whether something is an emergency.
- Missed escalation can become an expensive liability issue.
- Vendor dispatch is slower when photos, unit details and access instructions are incomplete.

## MVP Value Proposition

- Tenant intake form/SMS-style flow that requests photos and key details.
- Urgency classification with human override.
- Missing-information prompts tailored by category.
- Vendor message drafts with tenant-safe summaries.
- Admin metrics for aging, emergency volume, repeat issues and SLA risk.

## Section Copy

### Built for lean teams

Designed for operators who do not have a dedicated maintenance call center. One inbox, one triage flow and clear next actions.

### Emergency-aware, human-approved

The system highlights likely emergencies and safety risks, but property managers approve vendor dispatch and tenant messaging.

### Money-focused validation

Pilot success is measured by faster emergency response, fewer duplicate back-and-forth messages and reduced time from tenant report to complete work-order draft.
